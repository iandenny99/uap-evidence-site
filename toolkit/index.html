Journalist’s Toolkit

A quick-start pack for reporters. Use this to plan interviews, verify documents, file FOI requests, and map a fast investigation. Collaborate with specialists rather than duplicating work.

Quick Research Checklist

  • Identify the primary sources (transcripts, FOIA docs, datasets, peer-reviewed papers).
  • Log a simple timeline (who/what/when/where) and mark gaps to fill.
  • Separate fact vs claim vs critique in your notes.
  • Contact subject-matter experts (see Scientists) and witnesses for corroboration.
  • Liaise with Christopher Sharp at Liberation Times as the UK first port of call.

Interview Planning (fast)

  • Witnesses: ask about records shown, dates, other observers, sensor data, chain of custody.
  • Scientists: ask about methods, controls for artefacts, replication attempts, open data.
  • Officials/Gatekeepers: ask about policy, scope of searches, why certain leads weren’t pursued.

FOI / Records Requests — outline

Keep it narrow, date-bounded, and search-friendly. Example outline:

Dear [Authority],

Under the Freedom of Information Act, please provide:
1) Copies of [specific reports/emails/minutes] concerning [topic/incident] between [date range];
2) Any search logs/terms used and directorate(s) consulted;
3) If exemptions apply, please specify which sections and why.

I prefer electronic copies. Many thanks,
[Name]

Tip: submit parallel requests to multiple relevant bodies; log reference numbers; diarise review dates.

Verification & Documentation

  • Archive important pages (PDF or web archive) and record access dates.
  • Keep a source list with contact details and consent notes (on/off record).
  • Note counter-claims and give right of reply where appropriate.
