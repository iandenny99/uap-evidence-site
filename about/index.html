About & Editorial Policy

Mission: assemble primary sources and serious leads so journalists and the public can evaluate claims about UAP with clear evidence, not rhetoric. Our theme is simple: You don’t need to be Sherlock Holmes to spot a pattern here.

Sourcing Standards

  • Primary documents first (official records, testimony transcripts, FOIA releases, datasets).
  • Peer-reviewed research and formal studies where available.
  • Official statements from government/defence/intelligence bodies.
  • Reputable journalism with transparent sourcing.
  • Commentary/analysis clearly labelled as such.

Each item separates fact, claim, and critique; links counter-claims; and states a status: Un-debunked, Disputed, or Debunked (with source).

People Classifications

  • Whistleblowers & Witnesses — first-hand testimony.
  • Gatekeepers — individuals/institutions documented as blocking or shaping access to information or legislation.
  • Journalists — reporters with serious investigations; BBC: first port of call is Christopher Sharp (Liberation Times).
  • Academics & Scientists — peer-reviewed or structured research (e.g., Villarroel, Nolan, Loeb).

Legal & Accuracy

  • We avoid defamation: entries are factual and sourced; contested points are marked and linked to counter-claims.
  • Corrections welcome. If you spot an error, provide the source and we’ll review and update.
