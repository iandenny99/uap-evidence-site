Gatekeepers (US & UK)

This page highlights figures and institutions frequently cited by journalists, lawmakers, and advocates as obstructing or slowing UAP transparency. Inclusion is based on public records and reporting (linked below) and is presented as a starting point for legitimate journalistic inquiry.

Important: Summaries below reflect claims made in sources we link. Readers should review those materials directly. If you’re named here and wish to provide a statement, please contact us.

United Kingdom — Institutional Gatekeeping

Unlike the U.S., the UK has no prominently named individual “gatekeepers.” Instead, long-standing institutional policy functions as the gate: the Ministry of Defence ceased investigating UFO/UAP reports in 2009 and has repeatedly stated there are no plans to re-establish a dedicated team. That stance keeps the issue below the political/media radar.

  • Parliamentary answer (Dec 2024): MoD ceased UAP work in 2009
  • House of Lords debate (June 2021)
  • Janes: UK Govt says UAPs pose no military threat

For reporters: Focus FOI on policy, procurement, and inter-agency coordination. Ask: who receives UK military UAP incident reports today? What is the escalation path? Which offices interface with allies on UAP data?
